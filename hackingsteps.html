Steps in Ethical Hacking

Reconnaissance, Scanning, Exploitation, and Clearing Footprints Logs are the four important steps in ethical hacking.

1. Reconnaissance

Reconnaissance is the first step in ethical hacking. It involves gathering information about the system and the victim. This step is important because it helps the hacker to understand the system and find vulnerabilities. The information can be gathered through social engineering or other means.

2. Scanning

Scanning is the second step in ethical hacking. It involves scanning the system of the victim to find vulnerabilities. In this step, the hacker will find the IP address, domain name, operating system information, and which ports are opened on the victim's system. The goal is to extract the right information from the gathered data.

3. Exploitation

Exploitation is the third step in ethical hacking. It involves gaining access to the system by using different types of exploits or tools. The hacker will hack the system and gain access to the information they want to retrieve.

4. Clearing Footprints Logs

The final step in ethical hacking is clearing footprints logs. In this step, the hacker removes all the records, history, and other traces of their activities. This is done to avoid being tracked by cybersecurity experts.

Remember, ethical hacking requires years of hard work and a deep understanding of various technologies and topics. It's not something that can be learned by watching a few videos or reading a few articles.
